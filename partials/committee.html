Overnight Host Program ucb.swe.admit@gmail.com

Chair: Sarah Spivack

The Overnight Host Program (OHP) Committee works to plan and implement a two-day event in which newly-admitted female engineering students travel to the UC Berkeley campus in order to experience life at Cal! Throughout the program, the admits will meet with professors, lab groups, and student organizations on campus, stay overnight with an undergraduate student host, and experience Cal Day!

Public Relations ucb.swe.pr@gmail.com

Chairs: Emily Zhon, Yitian Zou

The Public Relations committee is in charge of keeping SWE members in the loop about SWE events and opportunities. The main ways in which we do this are through preparing and distributing the weekly SWEmail, producing the newsletter that is distributed at general meetings, flyering around campus, chalking messages in classrooms, and posting social media updates including Facebook. If you have a passion for writing, design, communications/social media, or just an interest in public relations then we would love to have you join our team! (No experience necessary.)

Shadow an Engineer

Chairs: Asena Yildiz, Purva Gupta

Shadow an Engineer is SWE’s externship program that provides undergraduate engineering students an opportunity to experience what it is like to be a full-time engineer at a company over Spring Break. We are looking for dedicated individuals to help put together our program this semester by directly communicating with companies, working on the student application, or helping publicize the program.

Career Options In Engineering

Officers: Varsha Venkat, Smita Balaji

Career Options in Engineering is a series of events that introduces engineering students to “alternative” career paths — careers that are not typically associated with engineers, like consulting and patent law. As a committee member, you will be in charge of one of the events in our series; this entails corresponding with a company representative or student representative to discuss event ideas, and handling event logistics and promotion. Career Options is a new program, so you will have a lot of flexibility in terms of reaching out to new partner companies and planning your event. This committee is the perfect opportunity for anyone who wants to network and help plan all aspects of an event from the ideation to publicity stages!

Mini University

Officer: Tanisha Randhawa

MiniU is an outreach event for high school students considering majoring in STEM related fields, with special consideration and emphasis for those from low-income backgrounds. The goal of the program is to get participants interested in engineering related careers and inspire them to pursue new experiences. The apprentice will meet with me about once every other week up through the day of the event to help go over coordination and details.

Engineering Day

Officer: Kelly Chen

Engineering Day is a fun event in October where parents are able to drop by with their K-3rd graders and interact with exciting Halloween-themed science booths in a casual, carnival style. In past years, we have had over 100 kids attend!
