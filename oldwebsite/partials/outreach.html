Outreach

UC Berkeley's SWE Outreach committee is committed to helping kids develop a passion for science and engineering. We hold events for students in grades K through 12. Read more to find out how you can participate:


Mini University

Mini University is an event that provides Bay Area high school students with the opportunity to experience a day in the life of a Berkeley Engineering student. The goal of Mini-U is to expose first-generation, low-income, and minority students to careers in STEM fields through activities such as lab and campus tours, engineering activities, industry speakers, and student panels. Mini-U is an all-day event that takes place once in both the Fall and Spring semesters. For more information, please see the 'Mini-University' tab under 'Outreach' or send an email to swe.miniu@gmail.com.


Engineering Day

Engineering Day is a fun event in October where parents are able to drop by with their K-3rd graders and interact with exciting Halloween-themed science booths in a casual, carnival style. In past years, we have had over 100 kids attend! For more information, contact swe.engineering.day@gmail.com.


High School Engineering Program

The High School Engineering Program is a 10-week long program designed to introduce female high school students to the principles and applications of engineering. Through the course of the program, the students receive engaging lessons pertaining to various fields of engineering and are mentored in small teams to design and carry out an involved engineering project.


SWE Science Saturdays

SWE Science Saturdays are a series of activities hosted once every month that teach engineering ideas and principles to 4th and 5th grade students. SWE Science Scholars is for 6th - 8th graders. The students work in groups to complete different hands-on projects using their creativity and knowledge. Some examples of previous activities include creating catapults and building “earthquake-proof” structures out of classroom materials such as popsicle sticks, rubber bands, straws, and chopsticks.


Past Events

Junior Solar Sprint Challenge (JSSC):

JSSC is an annual one day event where 6th through 8th grade students race solar powered cars at the Lawrence Hall of Science. Students build and design their solar powered cars from a starter kit in the weeks leading up to the event. Their cars are judged by members of CalSol, UC Berkeley's solar vehicle team.
