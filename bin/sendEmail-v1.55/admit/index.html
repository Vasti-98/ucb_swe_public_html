About The Program

The New Admit Overnight Host Program, on April 19th-20th 2013, is a unique program specifically for women admitted to Berkeley’s College of Engineering for Fall 2013. The program provides female admits the opportunity to visit the Cal campus, sit in on a lecture by a Berkeley engineering professor, participate in engaging activities, attend a banquet with faculty and professors, and sleep overnight in university housing to experience life as a Berkeley engineer first hand. Participants are encouraged to also attend CalDay on April 20th. Invited admits who would like to attend should click the REGISTER tab and fill out the registration form. Space is limited so registrations will be accepted on a first-come, first-serve basis. Berkeley students who are interested in hosting admits overnight on Friday, April 19th, or volunteering for Friday’s and Saturday’s activities - please click here to find out more information and to volunteer.

About SWE

The Society of Women Engineers (SWE) was founded in 1950 with the mission to stimulate women to achieve full potential in careers as engineers and leaders, expand the image of the engineering profession as a positive force in improving the quality of life, and demonstrate the value of diversity. Visit http://swe.org for more information and we hope that you too will continue to aspire, advance, and achieve.

Information

Directions To Berkeley

Program check-in and checkout will take place at Bechtel building on the UC Berkeley campus. Bechtel can be found on the online campus map in B5. Driving directions can be found at the Berkeley visitors webpage.

Parking

Due to limited parking availability, we recommend that you use public transportation whenever possible. The closest parking garages to Bechtel are the Lower Hearst Structure and the Upper Hearst Structure.

Flying

If you are flying to Oakland or SFO airport, you can:
- Take a taxi from the airport to the UC Berkeley Campus
- Take an airport shuttle to campus
- Take the BART. (If coming from Oakland, you will have to take the AirBART bus to the BART station) Get off the BART at the Downtown Berkeley Station and the walk to campus is less than half a mile. On the day of the program, we will meet participants at the BART station to accompany participants to the program.

Train

Take the Amtrack to Richmond where the Amtrack station is connected to the BART station. Take the BART to the Downtown Berkeley Station and the walk to campus is less than half a mile. On the day of the program, we will meet participants at the BART station to accompany participants to the program.

Check in and Check out

Check in will be from on April 19th, 9:00am - 10:00am in Bechtel. Bring all sleeping bags and luggage to the check in desk. We will store belongings in Bechtel until it is time to meet your overnight host.

Check out will be be on April 20th, 9:00am - 4:00pm in Bechtel. You will bring your sleeping bags and luggage to breakfast and we will store your belongings while you attend CalDay. Return anytime to Bechtel before 4:00pm to have a parent sign you out (if you are under 18) and to collect your belongings.

What to Bring


Please pack light, since you will be bringing your luggage to/from your hosts’ place.
- Pillow & Sleeping bag
- Money for personal use
- Toiletries
- Comfortable clothes for Friday and Saturday
- Business casual clothes for banquet (you will have the opportunity to change before and after the banquet)
- Completed Waiver of Liability and Emergency Information forms
- All your questions about Cal, the college of engineering and life as a university student in general!

Forms

All participants must fill out the Waiver Of Liability and Emergency Information forms to participate in the New Admit Overnight Host Program. You will not be allowed to check in without these completed! Once you have received a confirmation email that you are registered or on the wait-list, fill out these forms.
